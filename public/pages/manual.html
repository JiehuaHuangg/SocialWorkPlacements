Introduction

The UWA Social Work Placement Mapper is a comprehensive tool designed to help the UWA Social Work Field Education team efficiently manage student placements. This system provides:

  • Geographic Visualization - View students, agencies, and staff on an interactive map
  • Advanced Filtering - Filter stakeholders based on multiple criteria
  • Automated Matching - Generate optimal placement recommendations
  • Data Management - Upload and manage student, agency, and staff data

This user manual will guide you through all aspects of the system, from logging in to exporting final placement matches.

System Overview

The system consists of four main components:

Login & Authentication

Secure access to the system with UWA credentials

Data Upload

Upload Excel files with student, agency, and staff information

Map Visualization

Interactive map with filtering capabilities

Matching System

Automated placement matching with customizable priorities

Login & Authentication

Accessing the System
  1. Navigate to the UWA Social Work Placement Mapper login page
  2. Enter your UWA email address (must end with @uwa.edu.au)
  3. Enter your password
  4. Click the "Login" button
Only @uwa.edu.au email addresses are permitted to access the system.
Creating an Account

If you don't have an account yet:

  1. Click the "Sign Up" button on the login page
  2. Enter your full name
  3. Enter your UWA email address
  4. Create a password (minimum 6 characters)
  5. Confirm your password
  6. Click the "Sign Up" button
New accounts will require verification from email before access is granted.

Data Upload

The Data Upload section allows you to upload Excel files containing information about students, agencies, and sessional staff. This data is essential for the visualization and matching features.

All four data categories (FE1 Students, FE2 Students, Agencies, and Sessional Staff) must be uploaded before you can use the visualization and matching features.
Upload Process Overview

The upload process consists of four steps:

  1. Upload FE1 Students Excel file
  2. Upload FE2 Students Excel file
  3. Upload Agencies Excel file
  4. Upload Sessional Staff Excel file
Step 1: FE1 Students Upload
Required Columns

Your Excel file must include these columns:

  • Name
  • Location
  • Sector Exception
  • Interested sectors
  • Domestic / International
  • Driver's Licence
  • First Nations (Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islanders)
  • UWA FE Coordinators
Column names must match exactly as shown above.
Upload Instructions
  1. Click "Browse Files" or drag and drop your Excel file
  2. Review the file preview to ensure data is correct
  3. Click "Upload File"
  4. Wait for the upload to complete
  5. Click "Next Step" to proceed

Map Visualization

The Map Visualization page displays all stakeholders (students, agencies, and sessional staff) on an interactive map. This allows you to visualize the geographic distribution and apply various filters to analyze the data.

Map Interface Components
  • Main Map - Interactive Mapbox map showing all stakeholders
  • Left Sidebar - Contains filters for stakeholder type, driver's license, student type, region, and gender
  • Right Sidebar - Contains filters for sectors/areas of interest
  • Search Bar - Allows searching by name or suburb
  • Map Legend - Shows the meaning of different marker colors and shapes
Using Filters
Left Sidebar Filters
  • Stakeholder Type - Filter by FE1 Students, FE2 Students, Agencies, or Sessional Staff
  • Driver's Licence - Filter by license type (International, Australian, or No license)
  • Student Type - Filter by International, Domestic, or First Nation students
  • Region - Filter by geographic region (North, Central, South, East, West, Rural)
  • Gender - Filter by gender

Click the "Reset All" button at the bottom of the sidebar to clear all filters.

Right Sidebar Filters

The right sidebar contains filters for sectors or areas of interest. These are dynamically populated based on the data in your uploaded files.

Click on a sector to filter the map to show only stakeholders associated with that sector.

You can combine filters from both sidebars to narrow down your search.
Search Function

The search bar at the top of the map allows you to quickly find specific stakeholders:

  1. Enter a name or suburb in the search box
  2. Click the search button or press Enter
  3. The map will zoom to the matching stakeholder(s)
The search function works with partial matches, so you don't need to type the full name.
Map Legend

The map legend helps you identify different types of stakeholders on the map:

  • FE1 Students
  • FE2 Students
  • Agencies
  • Sessional Staff

Click on any marker on the map to view detailed information about that stakeholder.

Matching System

The Matching System automatically pairs students with agencies based on various factors including location proximity, sector interests, and specific constraints. This powerful tool helps optimize student placements while considering multiple variables.

Matching Interface Components
  • Data Source Selection - Choose between using uploaded data or manual file upload
  • Matching Weights - Adjust the importance of location vs. sector interests
  • Results Tabs - View results in Map View, Results List, or Unassigned Students
  • Export Function - Export matching results to Excel
Setting Matching Weights

The matching algorithm balances two main factors:

  • Location Proximity - How close students are to agencies
  • Sector Interests - How well agency sectors match student interests

Use the slider to adjust the relative importance of these factors:

  • Move left for Location Priority - Prioritize placing students closer to their location
  • Move right for Sector Priority - Prioritize matching students with their interested sectors

Viewing Matching Results

After clicking "Run Matching Algorithm," the system will process the data and display results in three different views:

1. Map View

Shows all stakeholders on the map with lines connecting matched students and agencies. The map includes:

  • Color-coded markers for different stakeholder types
  • Connection lines between students and assigned agencies
  • Connection lines between students and assigned staff (LO and EFE)
  • Search function to find specific stakeholders
  • Toggle options to show/hide different types of connection lines
2. Results List

Shows detailed information about each match, including:

  • Overall match score
  • Individual student-agency match scores
  • Breakdown of location and sector scores
  • Distance between student and agency
  • Assigned Liaison Officers (LO) and External Field Educators (EFE)
  • Manual assignment controls for adjusting matches
3. Unassigned Students

Lists students who couldn't be matched with agencies, including:

  • Student details
  • Reason for being unassigned
  • Manual assignment controls to assign these students
You can manually adjust assignments using the dropdown menus in the Results List and Unassigned Students tabs.
Exporting Matching Results

To export the matching results to Excel:

  1. Go to the Results List tab
  2. Click the "Export Results to Excel" button
  3. Save the Excel file to your computer

The exported Excel file contains multiple sheets:

  • Summary - Overall statistics about the matching process
  • FE2 Students - Detailed information about FE2 student matches
  • FE1 Students - Detailed information about FE1 student matches
  • Unassigned Students - Information about students who couldn't be matched
  • Sessional Staff Summary - Details about staff assignments and capacity

Troubleshooting

Problem: Excel file upload fails or shows errors.

Solutions:

  • Ensure your Excel file has the exact column names as specified in the requirements
  • Check that your Excel file is in .xlsx or .xls format
  • Verify that your Excel file doesn't contain any password protection or macros
  • Try saving your Excel file as a new file and uploading again

Problem: Map doesn't load or markers don't appear.

Solutions:

  • Check your internet connection, as the map requires an active connection
  • Try refreshing the page
  • Ensure your browser is up to date
  • Try clearing your browser cache and cookies
  • Verify that all required data has been uploaded successfully

Problem: Matching algorithm produces unexpected results or too many unassigned students.

Solutions:

  • Adjust the matching weights to prioritize different factors
  • Check agency "Available spots" to ensure there are enough placements
  • Verify that student locations are correctly entered and can be geocoded
  • Check for specific constraints that might be preventing matches (e.g., sector exceptions)
  • Use the manual assignment controls to adjust problematic matches

Problem: Unable to log in or access the system.

Solutions:

  • Ensure you're using a valid @uwa.edu.au email address
  • Check that your password is correct
  • Try resetting your password
  • Clear browser cookies and try again
  • Contact the support if problems persist : Send mail

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I update the data?

A: It's recommended to update the data at the beginning of each placement cycle or whenever there are significant changes to student, agency, or staff information.

Q: Can I edit data after uploading?

A: The current system doesn't support direct editing of uploaded data. To make changes, you'll need to update your Excel files and re-upload them.

Q: How does the matching algorithm handle constraints?

A: The algorithm considers several constraints, including:

  • Student sector exceptions
  • Agency exceptions (e.g., international students, driver's license requirements)
  • FE2 students not being placed in the same sector as their FE1 placement
  • Students without FE1 onsite supervisors needing agencies with onsite supervisors
Q: What do the LO and EFE roles mean?

A: LO (Liaison Officer) and EFE (External Field Educator) are roles assigned to sessional staff:

  • LO (Liaison Officer) - Provides support and liaison between the university and the placement
  • EFE (External Field Educator) - Provides supervision when an agency doesn't have an onsite social work supervisor
Q: How are sessional staff assigned to students?

A: Sessional staff are assigned based on:

  • Geographic proximity to the student
  • Sector expertise matching the agency's sector
  • Available capacity (number of students they can support)
  • Role requirements (LO vs. EFE)
Q: Can I manually adjust the matches?

A: Yes, after running the matching algorithm, you can manually adjust assignments using the dropdown menus in the Results List and Unassigned Students tabs.

Q: What does the match score represent?

A: The match score (0-100) represents how well a student-agency pairing meets the criteria, considering:

  • Location proximity (weighted according to your slider setting)
  • Sector interest alignment (weighted according to your slider setting)
  • Higher scores indicate better matches
